I am passionate about the need for knowledge transfer and actively work with a student-run organization - DJ Unicode. It is an initiative to foster a culture of open-source development to students in their early years at college. I mentor students in backend web technologies and guide them in advanced CS subjects such as Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing and Computer Vision. Under Unicode, in my junior year I was the project lead of DJ Archive, and mentored the rest of the students in their projects. Another project that we deveoped was the College Event manager that is being used by the faculties right now in the college. We also conducted workshops for second and first year students where we taught multifarios topics including Python, HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, Object Oriented Programming and Django so that they can take part in Hackathons. Unicode has grown from 45 developers in its first year to roughly 70 developers currently with many deployed and ongoing projects.
